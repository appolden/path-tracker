Town guide

Accommodation & town guide

A town guide for the GR10 trail. The guide includes details of accommodation (hotels, bed & breakfast, gites), supermarkets and public transport.

The information in this guide has been collated from guide books, internet research and google maps. I will be walking the GR10 between June and August 2018. On completion and my return to real life, I will update this guide with my personal experiences.

Disclaimer: contained in this guide there are affilated links to booking.com, but you are in no way obligated to use these links.

The towns are listed in a west to east direction.

Hendaye

Most hotels in Hendaye can be found close to the beach. There are many campgrounds to the east. There are couple of hotel options close to the railway station.

  • Hotel de La Gare
  • La Palombe Bleue

The Basque village of Biriatou is situated on the side of Xoldokogaina (486m). Look out for events held on the pelota pitch.

  • Appts maison Onbor Zilo

Col d'Ibardin

The border between France and Spain runs through Col d'Ibardin. You will find many restaurants and supermarkets at Spanish prices.

  • Casa Rural Erreteneko borda

Olhette

There are two gites and one hotel. The Gîte Manttu-Baita is located next to the GR10 path. The hotel restaurant Trabenia can be found further down the road next to the main road.

  • Hôtel Restaurant Trabenia - +33 5 59 54 01 91
  • Chambres d'hôtes/Gîte Manttu-Baita - +33 5 59 54 46 72
  • Chambres d'hôtes/Gîte Trapero Baïta - +33 5 59 54 42 59
  • B&B Gypsie

Sare

Tourist office, campground and many small shops. There is a bus service from Sare to Saint-Jean-de-Luz. Saint-Jean-de-Luz is located 12kms north of Hendaye and has a train station

Chambres d'hôtes/Gite Hego Alde

The GR10 passes right in front of the house. This gite can be found approximately 3km from Sare and 9km before Ainhoa.

  • Chambres d'hôtes/Gite Hego Alde - +33 5 59 54 60 41

Ainhoa

Tourist village. There are no large supermarkets but you will be able to buy local Basque produce.

  • Chambres d'Hôtes Maison Tartea - +33 5 59 03 82 38
  • Chambres d'hôtes Xaharenea - +33 5 59 29 90 72
  • Hôtel-restaurant La Maison Oppoca - +33 5 59 29 90 72

  • Hôtel-Restaurant Argi Eder - +33 5 59 93 72 00

Col des Veaux

Venta Burkaitz is a bar-restaurant with accommodation available. Check opening times because it may only be open in summer.

  • Venata Burkaitz - +33 5 59 29 82 55

Gîte Urrizate

The Gite Urrizate is situated next to the GR10 path just before reaching Bidarray.

  • Gîte Urrizate - +33 5 59 37 74 76

Bidarray

There is a train station that connects to Bayonne and Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port. Some accommodation can be found on the other side of the river Nive, such as the Le Chalet d'Iparla (1.4km from the GR10). Call ahead and they might come to pick you up.

If you have the time, then visit Noblia bridge which spans the Nive. The legend tells it was built by witches, which is why it's locally referred to as the "the Bridge of Hell"

  • L'Auberge Iparla - +33 5 59 37 87 09
  • Hôtel Barberaenea - +33 5 59 37 74 86
  • Gîte Aire Zabal - +33 6 60 81 09 20
  • Le Chalet d'Iparla

Search for accommodation in Bidarray

St Etienne de Baigorry

Tourist information center. Bakery Irastorza (closed on weekends). The Intermarche supermarket and campground are situated north of the tourist office. For the hotels and gites continue over the bridge that crosses the Nive des Aldudes.

  • Hôtel-restaurant Juantorena - +33 5 59 37 40 78
  • Hôtel-restaurant Arcé
  • Gite Gaineko Karrikan - +33 5 59 37 47 04
  • Camping Municipal Irouleguy

Search for accommodation in St Etienne de Baigorry

Lasse

Lasse is a small hamlet centered around a crossroad. It is located 2.5 kms before reaching the large town of Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port. If you want a quieter night then Lasse is the better option.

  • Auberge Etchoinia
  • Gîte de France - +33 5 59 37 07 41
  • Gîte de groupe de la Cidrerie Aldakurria - +33 6 70 94 60 57

Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port

Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port is also a starting point for pilgrims travelling the Camino de Santiago. Also know as, The Way of St James. Look out for the scallop shells that are used as way markers for the Camino. Spend an evening with some of the pilgrims discussing what the shells represent and gloat about how much tougher the GR10 is to walk than the Camino.

Monday is market day with sheep and cattle driven into the town. At 5pm, there is a communal game of bare-handed pelote at the fronton.

There are many accommodation options, tourist office and two supermarkets. The train station connects with Bayonne.

  • Hotel des Remparts
  • Gîte d'etape Compostella
  • Gîte d'étape Zuharpeta
  • Gite Makila
  • Hôtel des Pyrénées

Search for accommodation in Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port

Caro

The small village of Caro is on the GR10, approximately 3km after leaving Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port. The Maison Florenia is the closest accommodation to the GR10 trail.

  • Maison Florenia - +33 6 37 71 49 00

Estérençuby

Estérençuby has a couple of hotel-restaurants. The hotel Andreinia Logis can be reached by crossing the bridge over the Nive de Béhérobie.

  • Hôtel Andreinia Logis
  • Auberge Carricaburu - +33 5 59 37 09 77

Gîte d'étape Kaskoleta

The gîte Kaskoleta is located on the plateau Phalgacette next to the GR10. Ask permission if you want to camp here.

  • Gîte d'étape Kaskoleta

Logibar

There is only one option of accommodation at Logibar, the Auberge Logibar. However, it is located next to the D26 road so it may be possible to hitch to Larrau, 2.5km west of Logibar. In Larrau there is a gite, campground and a couple of hotels.

  • Auberge Logibar - +33 5 59 28 61 14

Refuge Jeandel

The Refuge Jeandel is located near to the Arette la Pierre Saint Martin ski station. It has full refuge facilities and sometimes stocks basic provisions.

As with all ski resorts there is no guarantee that all the facilities will be open in summer. If you arrive in high summer you should find that the tourist information centre and the bar-restaurant are open.

There is a bus service to Oloron-Saint-Marie (journey time approximately 1 hour). Oloron-Saint-Marie has a train station which connects to the main rail network.

  • Refugee Jeandel - +33 5 59 66 14 46

Lescun

Located in the Pyrénées National Park Lescun's old buildings and narrow streets, exudes a pleasant old village charm. The shop is open M-S 08:30–13:00 16:30–19:00.

11km north of Lescun there is a larger town called Bedous. In Bedous you will find ATMs, a pharmacy, tourist information centre and a train station. There doesn't appear to be any public transport available between Lescun and Bedous, so its time to get the thumb out and hitch if you need any of its facilities.

  • Hébergement du Pic d'Anie - +33 5 59 34 71 54
  • Gîte Dubois - +33 6 83 04 22 79
  • Refuge de L'Abérouat - +33 5 59 34 71 67

Gîte d'étape et camping Lhers

The gite de Lhers is located is pasture land 6km after Lescun. There is also a campground if you prefer a night under canvas. On occasion bread, pastries, drinks and will be on sale.

  • Gîte d'étape de Lhers - +33 5 59 34 75 39

Borce

Borce and Estaut are located on either side of RN134. Borce will be reached first if you are walking from the west. In Borce you can quench your thirst in the Le Communal bar, which has an attached gite. If you want a change of scenery then visit the wildlife park (Parc'Ours). It's your best chance of seeing a bear in the Pyrenees.

Some guides suggest that there is a bus service that connects to Pau but I haven't been able to find any evidence of this during my research.

  • Gîte Le Communal - +33 5 59 34 86 40
  • Chambre d'hôtes Maison Bergoun - +33 6 42 69 47 45

Etsaut

Cross the bridge over the RN134 (while remember the feeling of being in a car) to enter Etsaut. Head straight to le Randonneur bar in the main square for a much needed refreshment. Analysis of google Streetview, suggests that there is a bus stop next to this bar.

  • Chambres d’Hôtes La Maison d’en Bas - +33 6 32 91 55 23
  • Gîte Auberge La Garbure - +33 5 59 34 88 98

Gabas

Located in the Ossau valley, Gabas is just a few buildings next to the D934 road that connects to Laruns (14kms north). Google maps shows that there is a bus stop here but I can't find any details regarding the timetables.

  • Hôtel-restaurant Le Biscaü - +33 5 59 05 31 37
  • Hôtel-restaurant Vignau - +33 5 59 05 34 06

Gourette

Most of the facilities will be closed in the summer because Gourette is primarily a ski resort. I recommend that you check opening times before you start the day's stage.

  • Hôtel Le Glacier - +33 5 59 05 10 18
  • Hôtel Restaurant de l'Amoulat - +33 5 59 05 12 06

Arrens-Marsous

Arrens-Marsous is located in the Pyrénées National Park. The GR10 path passes to the south of Arrens-Marsous. Shortly after passing the Catholic church turn left to walk towards the town centre. In the town centre you will find the tourist information centre in the Maison du Val d'Azun.

There are two campgrounds which can be found to the north of the town near to the Proxi supermarket.

  • Gîtes Estives d'Arrens
  • Camping Mialanne - +33 5 62 92 67 14

Refuge d'Ilhéou

The Refuge d'Ilhéou is on the shore of the Lac Bleu d'Ilhéou (blue lake) in the heart of the Pyrénées National Park. Open from the beginning of June until October. Meals, snacks, breakfast available.

  • Refuge d'Ilhéou - +33 5 62 92 07 18

Cauterets

Cauterets is one of the largest towns along the GR10 trail. It is famous for being a spa town. It's an ideal place to spend a rest day if you legs are sore.

The historic market town of Lourdes is located 20km to the north. Lourdes is a major Catholic pilgrimage site. It is estimated that up to a million people visit the the Grotto of Massabielle. If your looking to go off-piste and hit up some culture then Lourdes is an ideal option. But after spending weeks in the mountains the hordes of people will come as a shock.

Search for accommodation in Cauterets

Chalet la Grange de Holle

A basic CAF refuge with mountain views, with a cozy dining room, terrace and a lounge. If you are unable to find accommodation here then follow the road down the hill to Gavarnie (1.5kms).

  • Chalet la Grange de Holle - +33 5 62 92 48 77

Gavarnie

The GR10 doesn't visit Gavarnie but if you want to visit then the take road down the hill from the Chalet-refuge la Grange de Holle (1.5kms).

Gavarnie is known for the Cirque de Gavarnie which is well worth a visit. Here you will find the Gavarnie Falls, which some claim to be the highest in Europe at 427 metres. The Cirque de Gavarnie and Gavarnie Falls are part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site Pyrénées – Mont Perdu.

As for most UNESCO sites, Gavarnie attracts many visitors. And when the GR10 crosses a popular destination you will find the all the facilities you might need. Gites, campgrounds, shops, restuarant and a tourist office.

There is a bus connection to Luz-Saint-Sauveur.

  • Hôtel Restaurant Le Marboré - +33 5 62 92 40 40
  • Gite d'étape Oxygène - +33 5 62 92 48 23
  • Hôtel Vignemale - +33 5 62 92 40 00
  • Hôtel des Cimes

Saint-Sauveur

Saint-Sauveur is found on the west side of the Gavarnie river. This side of the rive you will find thermal baths, hotels and gites but if your are looking for shops and restaurants then continue on to Luz-Saint-Sauveur.

  • Hôtel Panoramic et des Bains
  • Hôtel Ardiden
  • Les Gîtes du Pla de Moura

Luz-Saint-Sauveur

At an altitude of 711 m altitude, Luz-Saint-Sauveur is inside one of the most beautiful valleys of the Hautes-Pyrenees. In Luz-Saint-Sauveur, there are all the facilities you need. Supermarkets, hotels, campgrounds, food, drink and a tourist information centre.

There is a bus connection to Lourdes but I'm unable to determine the exact details of when and where the bus departs from. The bus operator is called, Keolis Pyrenees and there are two departures Monday to Friday.

  • Gîte Ste Barbe
  • Hôtel des Cimes
  • Gite d'etape le Regain

Search for accommodation in Luz-Saint-Sauveur

Barèges

Barèges is yet another Pyrenees spa town. Good selection of shops, restuarants and hotels included a outdoor gear shop and a cinema.

It is 8km by road to Luz-Saint-Sauveur. A possible option is to book two nights in Luz-Saint-Sauveur, slack pack the day's stage and hitch hike back.

  • Camping La ribere - +33 5 62 92 69 01
  • Gîte l'Oasis - +33 5 62 92 69 47
  • Logis Hôtel le Central
  • Chambres d'hotes lassalle cazaux **

Vielle-Aure/Saint-Lary-Soulan

The GR10 passes through the centre of Vielle-Aure. Its larger neighbour Saint-Lary-Soulan is located approximately 2km south of where the GR10 passes through this small village. In Vielle-Aure, there is a tourist office, gite a cafe and a restaurant. The large Carrefour supermarket is located a further 0.5km along the GR10.

Saint-Lary-Soulan has a wider selection of hotels and restaurants. Its also worth noting that there are couple of sporting goods stores. But expect to pay tourist prices. Maybe, ask for a GR10 hiker discount, you might get lucky.

  • Gîte Orédon - +33 6 89 96 50 60
  • Hôtel Aurélia **
  • Hôtel Mir ***
  • Gite Maison des Marmites ***

Tarascon-sur-Ariège

The town of Tarascon-sur-Ariège is location 10.5 km north of Siguer where you will find two supermarkets, hotels and a train station. There appears to be no bus service but hitch-hiking should be possible.

  • Le Manoir d'Agnès
  • Hôtel Confort
  • Hôtel Le Bellevue

Search for accommodation in Tarascon-sur-Ariège

Mérens-les-Vals

Mérens-les-Vals has a train station on the Latour-de-Carol and Toulouse line. The large town of Ax-les-Thermes is 9km to the north which can be reached by train.

  • Camping de Mérens - +33 5 61 02 85 40
  • Gite du soula
  • Maison Pinet Le Soula

Arles-sur-Tech

As you can probably guess from the name, this Catalan town lies next to the river Tech. There are ATMs, a supermarket, bakeries, and a few bar-restaurants to choose between. For all other enquires there is a tourist office.

If your legs are feeling weary at the point you can think about visiting the spas in the nearby town of Amélie-les-Bains-Palalda (4.5km north on the D115 road). In Amélie-les-Bains-Palaldam there is a wider selection of accommodation available, and several thermal baths to rejuvenate those tired legs.

  • Logis Hôtel les Glycines
